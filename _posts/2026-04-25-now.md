### reading _The Grapes of Wrath_

I am surprised and pleased to find that this classic – published almost exactly eightyseven years ago, in April of 1939 – is written in experimental style.
Steinbeck writes chapters in completely different modes.
Most the longer chapters he writes in conventional style, but he interleaves these with shorter chapters that abandon the main narrative to explore the greater context of the novel.
These smaller chapters are somewhat abstract and they are narrated through disembodied or multibodied voices.
They deal not with particular people but with the socioeconomic tides engulfing the novel's characters.
It's like Steinbeck wrote a novel and then went back to the beginning to weave through it a thread of commentary and imagery to reinforce and reflect the themes of the original book.
