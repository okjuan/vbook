Writing takes time.
There are many things to write about, too many.
I created this site – my "virtual book" – to help me write more.
More often, about anything, in smaller pieces.
I don't think smaller pieces are inherently better than long ones, but they are more practical to produce and to consume.

Writing is an art form.
An activity for producing things of beauty and for expressing things, ideas, feelings.
We idealize art.
It should be pure, it should transcend, it should be a thing all by itself.
But art is made by material beings in a physical world under the constraint of time and energy, within the logistical limits of existence.
We need to eat, we need to exercise, we need to rest, we need to go to work, we need to buy groceries, we need to clean.
We can only produce works of art in this environment, in this messy, awkward, inconvenient, frustrating, deflating reality.
The task of making art, of creating, is perhaps above all a logistical, practical one.
When?
How?
