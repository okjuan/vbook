
In the public consciousness there is moral weight to reading books.
Readers are seen an impressive people and nonreaders are ashamed of their lack of participation.
I don't read many books but I do read with some regularity and I like to think and talk about the books I read.
When I talk about a book I read, friends of mine who don't read routinely often make a quiet, passing comment to themselves that they should read more.

My enthusiasm for books and my tendency to bring them up in conversation makes me seem like I read a lot.
But I don't, really.
Last year I read twelve books, listened to one audiobook, and read chunks of four more books.

I do, however, engage deeply with the books I read.
I read them and they reshape my worldview or reinforce certain opinions.
